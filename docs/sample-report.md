## Overview

Born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, Elon Musk emigrated to Canada in 1988 and later studied physics and business at the University of Pennsylvania. He co-founded Zip2, which was sold to Compaq for $307 million, and later helped create X.com, which became PayPal — eventually acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion. Musk used the proceeds to launch SpaceX in 2002 and Tesla Motors in 2004. Over the years, he has expanded his empire to include The Boring Company, Neuralink, and xAI, and in 2022, he acquired Twitter, renaming it X. His companies collectively have a combined valuation exceeding $1.6 trillion, with SpaceX and Starlink emerging as key drivers of his wealth.

Musk's leadership style is often described as transformational, but also authoritarian. He is known for demanding long hours, micromanaging, and making high-profile decisions via social media — often without consulting employees. His public persona is marked by volatility, from grandiose pronouncements about AI and space colonization to inflammatory political statements and legal battles with regulators and advertisers.
